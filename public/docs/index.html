Purchase Orders

A Purchase Order (PO) is the formal commitment to buy goods or services from a vendor. This module covers raising a PO, getting it approved, issuing it to the vendor, and tracking work completion and payments.

Who can do what

Action Admin Finance Operations Viewer
View purchase orders ✓ ✓ ✓ ✓
Create & edit a PO ✓ — ✓ —
Update payment status ✓ ✓ ✓ —
Approve & issue PO to vendor ✓ — — —
Waive NDA / active-vendor requirement ✓ — ✓ —
Approve a waiver / completion certificate ✓ — — —
Delete a PO dev only — — —

Deleting a PO is restricted to the Superadmin (developer) role.

The Purchase Orders screen

Open Vendors → Purchase Orders from the sidebar. The top cards show how many POs exist and the total open (unpaid) amount. Use the search box and the Status, Vendor, and Project filters to narrow the list. Each row shows the PO number, vendor, linked project, amount, and current status. Click any row to open it; use the download icon to grab the PDF.

Workflow — Create a purchase order Admin · Operations

  1. Click “Create PO”

    Top-right of the Purchase Orders screen. This opens the Create Purchase Order form.

  2. Select the vendor

    Only vendors with an approved Signed NDA who are Active appear ready to go. Optionally link a Project.

  3. Describe the order & set dates

    Add a description/subject, the Issued Date, and an optional Due Date and Down Payment amount.

  4. Add line items

    Each row has an item code, description, quantity, unit of measure, and unit price. The amount totals automatically. The PO total must be greater than zero.

  5. Submit

    The PO is created as a Draft and then enters the approval flow below.

⛔Two gates before a PO can be raised The vendor must (1) have an approved Signed NDA on file and (2) be Active (Accredited). If either is missing, the system blocks creation with a clear message.
⚠️Waiving the gates Admin and Operations can tick Waive requirements to raise a PO anyway (e.g. urgent work). A waived PO is flagged and cannot be issued to the vendor until an Admin approves the waiver.

Workflow — Approve & issue to the vendor Admin approves

Opening a PO shows its full detail: status, the approval banner, completion certificates, and a financial summary (commitment, down payment, paid-to-date, balance, billing health).

  1. PO sits in “Pending Approval”

    After creation it awaits executive sign-off. It cannot be sent to the vendor in this state.

  2. An Admin reviews and clicks “Approve & Issue”

    This moves the PO to Issued — it can now be downloaded and sent to the vendor. Or click Reject to send it back.

  3. Download or edit the document

    Use Download for the PDF, or Edit DOCX to fine-tune the document before issuing.

⚠️Approval is mandatory No PO reaches a vendor without an Admin approving it first. This is the executive control layer over all outgoing commitments.

Workflow — Track completion & payments

  1. Vendor work is logged as a Completion Certificate

    Each certificate records a % complete and an optional uploaded file and notes.

  2. An Admin approves or rejects each certificate

    Approved certificates confirm progress (e.g. 50% complete) against the PO.

  3. Payments flow in from invoices

    The financial summary tracks original commitment, down payment, total paid-to-date, remaining balance, and invoiced-vs-PO billing health. As invoices are recorded and paid, the PO status advances (Partially Paid → Paid).

💡Where payments come from You don’t mark a PO “Paid” by hand — recording a payment against its invoice cascades back and updates the PO automatically. See the Invoices & Payments module.

Getting Started

TelcoVantage ERP is the single place to run procurement, finance, projects, and people. This page covers signing in and finding your way around — everything here applies to every role.

Signing in

  1. Go to the login page

    Open the ERP URL. You’ll see the sign-in screen.

  2. Sign in with Microsoft

    Click Sign in with Microsoft to use your company account — the recommended way. Or enter your email and password if you have a local account.

  3. You land on the Command Center

    The dashboard that opens depends on your role: you only ever see the modules your role is allowed to use.

💡Forgot your password?Use the reset link on the login screen. Microsoft sign-in users don’t need a password at all.

The Command Center dashboard

The home dashboard is your operational pulse: near-due invoices and POs (within 7 days), current liability, active POs, pending vendors, active projects, expiring documents, AR outstanding, and a 6-month cash-flow trend. The New PO and Record Invoice quick-actions are top-right.

Finding your way around

  • ⌘K / Ctrl-K search

    Press ⌘K (or click “Search everything…”) to jump to any vendor, PO, invoice, project, or customer instantly.

  • Notification bell

    The bell (top bar) shows alerts — approvals waiting, expiring docs, near-due items.

  • Theme toggle

    Switch between light and dark mode with the moon/sun icon.

  • Profile menu

    Top-right shows your name and role. Open it for your profile and to sign out.

  • AI Assistant

    The floating button (bottom-left) opens the assistant — see the AI Assistant section.

The five roles

Role What they do
Admin The director — runs the business. Can do almost everything, including approvals.
Finance Invoices, payments, accounting, and client billing.
Operations Vendors, POs, projects, CRM, contracts, and assets.
Viewer Read-only access across the system.
Vendor (external) Not a login — vendors use a one-time portal link to upload documents.

Throughout this guide, pick your role at the top to grey out anything you can’t do.

The invoices screen

Open Invoices from the sidebar. The list shows each invoice, its vendor, linked PO, amount, and payment status. Filter by status or vendor; click a row to open the detail.

Workflow — Record an invoice Admin · Finance

  1. Click “Record Invoice”

    From the dashboard quick-action or the Invoices screen.

  2. Pick the vendor and PO

    Link the invoice to the purchase order it bills against.

  3. Enter the invoice number, amount, and date

    Optionally attach the invoice file (max 10MB).

  4. Save

    The invoice is recorded against the PO.

⛔Amount guardThe total invoiced against a PO cannot exceed the PO amount. If an approved completion certificate exists, the ceiling is capped at that percentage of the PO (e.g. 50% complete → you can only bill up to 50% of the PO).

Workflow — Record a payment Admin · Finance

  1. Open the invoice and click “Record Payment”

    Enter the amount paid and the date.

  2. Save

    The invoice updates to Partially Paid or Paid.

💡Auto-cascadeYou never set a PO to “Paid” by hand. Recording payments against its invoices cascades back and advances the PO status automatically (Issued → Partially Paid → Paid).

AI Assistant

The TVPH Assistant is a chat helper that can analyse your vendors, POs, and financial data and answer questions in plain language.

Opening the assistant

Click the floating assistant button (bottom-left, on every page). The panel opens with suggested prompts. Type a question in “Ask your assistant…” and press send. You can expand the panel or clear the conversation from the header icons.

What you can ask

  • “Who are our top vendors?”

    Summaries and rankings across your supplier base.

  • “List pending POs”

    Pull up records by status without hunting through filters.

  • “What’s our compliance score?”

    Accreditation readiness across vendors.

💡It respects your dataThe assistant answers from the same records you can see in the system, so its analysis reflects live data.

Vendor Portal

How your vendors submit accreditation documents — a secure, login-free page they reach through a one-time link you generate.

1 · Generate the link (internal) Admin · Operations

Open the vendor’s profile and click Generate Portal Link. Copy the link and send it to your vendor contact. The link is time-limited for security.

2 · The vendor’s upload experience (external)

The vendor opens the link — no account needed — and sees their accreditation checklist: a progress ring (e.g. 1 / 14 files) and a card per required document marked Approved or Missing. They click a requirement to upload a file or draw an e-signature.

3 · Review (internal)

Each upload flows back into Compliance and the vendor’s Accreditation Docs tab, where an Admin or Operations user approves or rejects it. Once the Signed NDA is approved and the vendor is Active, you can raise POs for them.

💡E-signatureSome requirements (like the Statement of Commitment) let the vendor draw a signature directly in the portal instead of uploading a file.
